The article argues that detecting AI hallucinations after they occur is insufficient for production systems, since detection alone doesn't determine what happens next. It advocates for runtime enforcement layers—halt-and-escalate, graceful degradation, or rerouting—that intercept hallucinated outputs before they reach downstream systems.
